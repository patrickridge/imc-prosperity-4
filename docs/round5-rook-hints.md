# Round 5 — Rook-E1 Hints (Algo)

## 1. Cluster first ("It's a Lot")

50 products. Individual inspection is infeasible. Group them into clusters with shared structural characteristics. Look for categories that consistently outperform or underperform. Inspect meaningful groups, then drill into individual products within each.

## 2. Pair relationships ("Pairing for Profit")

Within strong clusters, examine *why* they perform. Do products move together or diverge predictably? Stable cross-product relationships reduce risk and strengthen conviction. Map the relationships, then test which ones hold.

## 3. Lead/lag timing ("Same but Slower")

Similar products may operate on different timelines — one leads, another follows with a measurable delay. Map the gap: how large, how consistent, does it hold across rounds or compress/expand. A leading product lets you anticipate the lagging product's move before it happens.

## 4. Fees matter ("Don't Forget the Fees")

Gross opportunity is not net profit. Incorporate all transaction fees before evaluating. High-turnover strategies need extra scrutiny — costs compound across volume. Fee structures may change based on invested volume — this should influence capital allocation. Measure performance after costs only.

## Suggested pipeline

cluster → find pairs → exploit lead/lag → evaluate net of fees
